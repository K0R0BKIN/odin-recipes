Lemon-glazed carrots

Description

Lemon-glazed carrots are a delightful side dish that combines the natural sweetness of carrots with the bright, tangy flavor of lemon. This simple yet elegant recipe enhances the carrots' taste, making it a perfect accompaniment to various main courses.​

Ingridients

  • 500g carrots, peeled and cut into evenly sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Zest and juice of one lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the Carrots: Place the peeled and cut carrots in a medium saucepan. Add enough water to cover them and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and simmer until the carrots are tender, about 8-10 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Glaze: In the same saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the honey, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is smooth and slightly thickened, about 2-3 minutes.​
  3. Glaze the Carrots: Return the cooked carrots to the saucepan with the glaze. Toss to coat the carrots evenly. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the glaze to thicken and adhere to the carrots.​
  4. Season and Serve: Season the glazed carrots with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Transfer to a serving dish, garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired, and serve warm.
